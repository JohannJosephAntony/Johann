A sustainable footwear concept built from upcycled Pakistani textile waste — proving that cross-border circular design can compete on the global stage.

Designed for the FedEx / Junior Achievement International Trade Challenge, this project reimagines the sneaker lifecycle from Lahore to Shenzhen. The upper is constructed from upcycled Pakistani textile waste, sourced from garment industry offcuts and deadstock, while manufacturing and assembly are anchored in China to leverage established footwear infrastructure and regional trade routes.

The concept targets a 60% reduction in raw material waste and a fully circular end-of-life pipeline: worn shoes are returned, shredded, and reborn as the next production run. No virgin fabrics. No landfill. Just loops — stitched across borders.

The prototype earned Finalist placement — recognised for its combination of material innovation, Pakistan-to-China supply-chain thinking, and commercial viability across cross-border trade scenarios.

A platform connecting individuals and corporations with farmers to rent trees — turning carbon offsetting into a transparent, traceable transaction.

Traditional carbon offsets are opaque. Rent A Tree makes them visible. Users browse a marketplace of real trees managed by partner farmers, rent them by the month, and watch their CO₂ sequestration accrue in real time through a live dashboard.

The platform bridges three constituencies: individuals who want personal climate action, companies seeking ESG-grade offsets, and farmers who earn recurring revenue for maintaining tree cover. It's offsetting reimagined as a service — not a guilt payment, but a relationship.

This placed Second at GIIS Progressio 2023, recognised for its innovative market mechanism and direct impact measurability.

GIIS RWCC Entrepreneurship Challenge · Second Place
GRAPHENE
HYDROGEN

A lightweight hydrogen-powered vehicle frame using graphene-reinforced composites — halving weight while doubling structural integrity.

Built for the GIIS RWCC Entrepreneurship Challenge, this concept vehicle replaces traditional steel chassis members with a graphene-infused polymer composite, achieving a 40% weight reduction without sacrificing crash safety ratings. The frame houses a compressed hydrogen tank and a compact fuel cell stack — the only emission is water.

The engineering thesis: weight is the enemy of efficiency. By reducing mass at the structural level, the vehicle requires less energy to move, extending range per kilogram of hydrogen. The graphene composite also offers superior thermal dissipation, protecting the fuel cell under load.

The project placed Second, cited for its material science depth and commercial feasibility as a near-term sustainable transport solution.

Coordinating operations, logistics, and team execution for C² STEM Racing 2025 — where every second of pit time is a calculated variable.

AI for the Elderly · Singapore
LOCAI
· CO-FOUNDER
Local AI · Global Care
Health Monitor
Daily Assistant

An AI literacy initiative designed to empower Singapore's aging communities and their caregivers — accessible, dignified, and deeply local.

LOCAI is an initiative born conviction: technology should be accessible to those who need it most. Singapore's rapidly aging population faces digital exclusion, and both seniors and caregivers often lack the confidence to engage with AI tools. LOCAI bridges this gap through hands-on education and training delivered in local languages and dialects, adapted to cultural context.

The initiative combines: guided AI workshops for seniors and caregivers to build practical digital skills, simplified tool training on everyday AI applications for health, communication, and daily tasks, and a peer support network that connects trained caregivers with elders to reinforce learning and foster confidence. All sessions are designed to be patient, jargon-free, and culturally sensitive.

The initiative is currently in early-stage development with pilot conversations underway with eldercare facilities in Singapore.
